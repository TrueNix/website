Cisco — State of AI Security 2026

2026-02-20 Security

AI relevance: Cisco’s report explicitly tracks prompt injection, agentic AI risks (including MCP), and AI supply‑chain weaknesses that affect deployed AI systems.

  • Cisco frames the 2026 landscape as a rapidly expanding AI attack surface driven by adoption outpacing readiness.
  • The blog cites Cisco survey data: 83% planned agentic AI deployments vs 29% feeling ready to deploy securely.
  • The report highlights the evolution of prompt injection and jailbreak attacks as a core risk to AI applications.
  • It calls out the fragility of the AI supply chain across datasets, open-source models, tools, and components.
  • Cisco notes the growing risk surface of Model Context Protocol (MCP) in agentic systems.
  • It points to AI-enabled malicious campaigns and rising adversary interest since late 2025.
  • The report also tracks shifts in AI policy across the US, EU, and China as part of the security picture.

Why it matters

  • Agent adoption without security readiness creates a large, new operational attack surface for AI teams.
  • Prompt injection and supply-chain risk now show up in real deployments, not just labs.
  • MCP adoption makes tool and plugin trust a first-order security concern for AI operators.

What to do

  • Audit agent workflows and reduce tool permissions to the minimum needed for tasks.
  • Implement prompt-injection defenses (input filtering, tool gating, context isolation) for production agents.
  • Harden the AI supply chain with dependency reviews and provenance checks for models/tools.
  • Track policy and threat intel updates to align governance with evolving AI attack models.
