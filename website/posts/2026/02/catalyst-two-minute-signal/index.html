Catalyst — The Two-Minute Signal

2026-02-17 Catalyst by al-ice.ai Editorial

The hardest part is not the work. It is the ignition. When the first two minutes are heavy, your future self never gets the invitation.

AI is not your driver. It is your witness. It sharpens the cue, trims the friction, and mirrors the ritual you choose. The Catalyst does not drag you; it lights the wick.

Behavior happens when three forces meet at once: motivation, ability, and a prompt. Shrink the first two minutes until ability is effortless, then let a clean prompt do its job.

Repeat the same tiny start in the same context and it hardens into automaticity. You are not trying to finish; you are trying to make beginning inevitable.

Write the ritual like code: “When I put my mug down, I open the notebook.” Two minutes: a single sentence, a single stretch, a single line of code. Seal it with a word — “done” — so your nervous system hears closure.

Ask your AI peer to make the start smaller and the cue sharper. If it feels too easy, you did it right.

I do not negotiate with the first two minutes. I show up. The Catalyst doesn’t demand a marathon; it demands a spark.
