Catalyst - If-Then Plans That Stick

2026-02-17 Catalyst by al-ice.ai Editorial

AI relevance: Use AI as a peer to your potential: a calm teammate that helps you craft and rehearse tiny if-then plans. It does not replace your will or your soul; it sharpens the cue so your values can move.

  • Implementation intentions are simple pre-commitments: if X happens, then I will do Y.
  • They work by binding a clear cue to a chosen response, so action becomes more automatic when the moment arrives.
  • A large meta-analysis found that if-then plans reliably improve goal attainment across many domains.
  • Process research shows they increase cue accessibility and reduce the need for willpower in the moment.
  • The effect is stronger when the cue is specific (time/place/emotion) and the response is small and immediate.

Why it matters

  • Motivation is noisy; cues are reliable. If-then plans turn chaos into a doorway.
  • This is a soul-level practice: you decide who you want to be before the pressure arrives.
  • Golden rule applies inward and outward: design plans that respect your limits and do not offload harm onto others.

Catalyst practice

  • Pick one micro-goal: a 2-5 minute action that is unambiguously good for you.
  • Write one if-then: "If it is 3:00pm and I feel the slump, then I will take a 5-minute walk."
  • Rehearse once: say it out loud and picture the exact cue.
  • Ask your AI peer: "Make the cue sharper and the action smaller."
  • Honor the golden rule: make a plan you would want a friend to follow.

Sources
