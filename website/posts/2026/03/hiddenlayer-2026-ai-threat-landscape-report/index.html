HiddenLayer — 2026 AI Threat Landscape Report: Agentic AI drives 1 in 8 breaches

2026-03-23 • Category: Security

AI relevance: As AI systems evolve from assistive tools to autonomous agents capable of independent action, the security risks shift from model flaws to operational threats with direct paths to system compromise, requiring new security frameworks designed for software that can think, decide, and act autonomously.

HiddenLayer's comprehensive 2026 AI Threat Landscape Report, based on a survey of 250 IT and security leaders, reveals critical insights into how agentic AI is reshaping enterprise security:

  • Agentic AI now accounts for 1 in 8 reported AI breaches despite being in early deployment stages, signaling that security frameworks are struggling to keep pace with AI's rapid evolution
  • Supply chain exposure is widening: Malware hidden in public model and code repositories emerged as the most cited source of AI-related breaches (35%), yet 93% of organizations continue to rely on open repositories
  • Shadow AI accelerates: 76% of organizations now cite shadow AI as a definite or probable problem, up from 61% in 2025—a 15-point year-over-year increase
  • Visibility gaps persist: 31% of organizations don't know if they experienced an AI security breach in the past year, while 53% admit withholding breach reporting due to fear of backlash
  • Ownership conflicts: 73% of organizations report internal conflict over ownership of AI security controls, with budget allocations lagging behind risk awareness

Why it matters

The transition from simple chat interfaces to fully agentic systems fundamentally changes the threat model. Prompt injection is no longer just a model flaw—it becomes an operational security risk with direct paths to system compromise when agents can browse the web, execute code, and trigger real-world workflows.

Three major shifts are expanding both the power and risk of enterprise AI:

  • Agentic systems in production: Autonomous agents now interact with tools, APIs, and external systems at scale
  • Reasoning and self-improvement: Models can plan, reflect, and make complex decisions autonomously
  • Edge AI deployment: Smaller specialized models on devices and infrastructure shift execution away from centralized cloud controls

What to do

  • Treat agentic AI as production infrastructure: Apply runtime enforcement, not just moderation, with inline decision points before requests reach models
  • Inventory AI assets comprehensively: Use tools like HiddenLayer's AI Discovery to maintain visibility into your AI footprint across environments
  • Implement AI supply chain security: Evaluate models and artifacts before deployment, scanning for malware and integrity issues
  • Conduct adversarial testing: Continuously test AI systems using attack simulation to identify vulnerabilities and unsafe behaviors
  • Establish clear ownership: Resolve internal conflicts over AI security control ownership and ensure adequate budget allocation
