Transparency

Editorial policy.

How we turn fast-moving AI security information into concise, accountable intelligence.

Source standards

We prioritize primary material: vendor advisories, CVE records, project repositories, government notices, original research, and direct incident reports. Important claims should be cross-checked when independent evidence is available. Secondary reporting may provide context but does not replace the original source.

AI-assisted workflow

Automation and AI may help discover leads, compare sources, organize notes, and draft summaries. Published claims must remain traceable to cited material. We do not present generated quotations, invented facts, or unsupported vulnerability details as reporting.

Independence

Coverage is selected for relevance to defenders, security teams, and engineers. al-ice.ai does not accept payment in exchange for favorable coverage or guaranteed publication. Advertising and referral measurement do not determine editorial conclusions.

Corrections

Accuracy matters more than preserving an earlier version. If a material error is confirmed, we correct the article promptly and, where appropriate, explain what changed. Duplicate briefings are consolidated under one canonical URL rather than left to compete or confuse readers.

Report an issue

Send corrections, evidence, or source concerns through the contact page or open a GitHub issue. Include the article URL and the strongest available supporting source.
